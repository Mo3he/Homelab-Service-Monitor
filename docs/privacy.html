Peekr Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 25, 2026

Peekr is a service monitoring app for your self-hosted homelab services and cloud API accounts. This policy explains what data the app uses, where it stays, and what it never does.

What Peekr does not do

  • Peekr does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data.
  • Peekr does not include analytics, crash-reporting SDKs, or advertising frameworks.
  • Peekr does not create or require a user account.
  • Peekr does not track you across apps or websites.

What data the app stores, and where

Everything Peekr stores stays on your device:

  • Service configuration (hostnames, ports, service types, display names) is saved to UserDefaults on your device.
  • Credentials (API keys, usernames, passwords) are stored exclusively in the iOS/macOS Keychain and are never written to iCloud or transmitted anywhere.
  • Status history and metric history are kept in UserDefaults on your device and are never backed up to any server.
  • Notification schedules and alert rules are stored on-device only.

Network access

Peekr makes outbound HTTP/HTTPS/TCP connections exclusively to the service addresses you enter. These are connections from your device directly to your own infrastructure or the cloud APIs you configure (such as GitHub or Anthropic). No traffic is routed through any Peekr server because no Peekr server exists.

Self-signed certificates

For services running on your local network with self-signed TLS certificates, Peekr provides an opt-in "Allow self-signed certificate" toggle per service. This is disabled by default and only bypasses TLS validation for the specific host you enable it on. It has no effect on any other connection.

Background refresh

Peekr can check your services in the background using iOS Background App Refresh. These checks connect only to the service addresses you have configured and produce local notifications on your device. No data leaves your device as a result of background refresh.

Notifications

Peekr sends local notifications only (generated on your device). There is no push notification server. Notifications require your explicit permission and can be disabled in iOS Settings at any time.

Children

Peekr does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised date. Because Peekr collects no data, changes are expected to be minor and administrative in nature.
